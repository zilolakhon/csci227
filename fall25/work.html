Code Reviews & Written Quizzes

Quizzes:

Every week, there will be a quiz to be taken in 1001E HN, on the lecture notes, reading, submitted programs, and programming assignments.
  • As the semester progresses, quizzes will include review questions as well as short programming exercises based on the homework.
  • Quizzes are on paper (echoing the style of the final exam and the first pass of many programming job interviews).
  • For the quizzes, you may not use any notes, books, or any computer device (laptop, phone, calculator, smart watch, etc.).
  • There are no make-up quizzes. Instead, the 10 highest quiz scores are used when computing the final grade.

Code Reviews:

Every week, there will be an in-person code review on the recently submitted programs.
  • For each code review, you choose one of the listed programs to explain on the whiteboard.
  • Code reviews are on the whiteboard (echoing the style of many programming job interviews).
  • All code reviews must be taken by the deadline.
  • There are no make-up code reviews. Instead, the 10 highest code review scores are used when computing the final grade.

Final Examination

The final exam is required. It is comprehensive, covering all the material of the course.

  • You must take and pass the final (60 points or more) to pass the course.
  • The final exam will be during the registrar's assigned time slot: TBD.

Exam Rules:

During the exam, you should have with you:

  • Your Hunter ID
  • One 8.5"x11" 2-sided page of notes (no origami, no additional pages)
  • Pencils or pens

During the exam:

  • All phones must be face down on the table in front of you.
  • All ear pods and ear phones must be removed.
  • No phones, computers, tablets, calculators, or other electronic devices may be used.
  • All notes and books must be put away in your backpack.

Exam Format:

On the day of the exam, there will be two parts to fill out:

  • Seat Slip: has your name printed on slip. Fill out the remaining information and sign the seat slip.
  • Final Exam: make sure to fill out the front page information.

Both the seat slip and the final exam must be submitted.


The final exam has 10 questions, each worth 10 points. The best way to study is to work through the problems we have done throughout the semester. The questions are very similar to the classwork, programming assignments, quizzes, examples from lecture and the reading.

On the last lecture, Wednesday, 12 December, we will have a mock final exam. It will be similar to the actual final exam, except that the time will be shorter, since lecture is 1 and 15 minutes while the final exam is 2 hours. An answer key will be available later that afternoon on Blackboard.
